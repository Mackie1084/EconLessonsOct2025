ü§î What is Price Elasticity of Demand?

Price elasticity of demand measures how responsive consumers are to price changes.

üìê Formula:

PED = % Change in Quantity Demanded / % Change in Price

üèÉ‚Äç‚ôÇÔ∏è

Elastic (PED > 1)

Consumers are very responsive to price changes

Example: Luxury cars, dining out

üêå

Inelastic (PED < 1)

Consumers are not very responsive to price changes

Example: Rice, electricity, gasoline

‚öñÔ∏è

Unitary (PED = 1)

Price and quantity changes are proportional

Example: Some consumer goods

üéØ Factors Affecting Price Elasticity

Several factors determine how elastic demand will be:

üîÑ

Availability of Substitutes

More substitutes = More elastic

Singapore: Many food options in hawker centers

India: Limited substitutes for staples like rice

üí∞

Income Level

Higher income = More elastic for luxuries

Singapore: Less concerned about basic food prices

India: Very sensitive to essential goods pricing

‚è±Ô∏è

Time Period

Long-run = More elastic than short-run

Both countries: Consumers adjust habits over time

‚ù§Ô∏è

Necessity vs Luxury

Necessities = Inelastic, Luxuries = Elastic

Context varies: Cars are luxury in India, necessity in Singapore

üìä

Proportion of Income

Higher % of income = More elastic

India: Food takes larger income share

Singapore: Housing takes largest share

üè†

Brand Loyalty

Strong loyalty = Less elastic

Both countries: Premium brands command loyalty
